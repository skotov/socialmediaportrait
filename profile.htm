        I'm shocked, numb, afraid, devastated. I don't know what to do, but here's a start:

        Eliza Cadoux
        November 9 at 11:13am · Ann Arbor, Ann Arbor ·

        To all the people who woke up this morning and thought "I don't know what to do," here's what you can do:

        – Donate money to organizations that promote inclusivi...ty, love and diversity.
        – Call and write your elected officials, starting with your city councilor and ending with your president.
        – Volunteer for a non-profit that aims to make this country better for all people—poor Americans, Muslim Americans, Black Americans, women, Latinos, immigrants, the homeless.
        – Subscribe to a newspaper or donate to a news organization. Help us keep powerful people accountable for their actions.
        – Encourage LGBT people, women and people of color to run for office, and then donate to and volunteer for them.
        – Vote. Not just in the presidential election, but in the midterms, in municipal elections, and everything in between.
        – Tell people that you believe them when they're sexually assaulted, harassed and abused.
        – Tell people of color that you believe them when they say that they are afraid for their lives and for their children's lives.
        – Tell Muslims that you welcome them, that you're not scared of them, and that you will stand up for everyone's Constitutional right to practice their faith of choice or no faith at all.
        – Tell LGBT people that you love them regardless of their gender or sexual orientation and that their lives are not worth less than the lives of straight people.

        Please do something. Please don't sit at home and sulk. There are people in this country who need you to stand up for them, and this is your chance. Please don't let everything you have worked and hoped for go to waste.

        (Copied from my beautiful friend Elise Jayakar)
